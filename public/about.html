Meet the founder

Hi, I'm Taleena.

20 year-old Computer Science & Cybersecurity student at The University of Western Australia

How It Started

A friend once mentioned the whale migration was passing Rottnest that week but by the time I actually thought about going, it was over. It wasn't the first time. I kept missing things that were happening right near me, the whole time, without ever knowing.

Growing up in regional WA, I often felt disconnected whether that be from nature, from opportunity, and sometimes from myself. Like a lot of people my age, I've had plenty of nights where I've gone to bed feeling like I didn't do enough that day, for myself or for the planet. It's sometimes called eco-anxiety; caring deeply about the future while feeling powerless against problems that seem too big for one person to fix.

I wanted to build something that turned that feeling into something gentler and more constructive. Tech became my way of bridging back to connection, to nature, and to a sense that my actions actually mattered.

Making It Happen

Syqei is built around something simple: every real place you visit a park, a reserve, a stretch of coastline, you unlock a real, scientifically accurate butterfly into your own personal garden. Butterflies were chosen from the very start as a symbol of transformation, resilience, and biodiversity.

Why "Syqei"

Syqei is pronounced psy-key which is a play on Psyche. In Greek mythology, Psyche was a mortal woman whose story became a symbol of the soul itself; her name, ψυχή, means both "soul" and "butterfly." The Ancient Greeks believed the butterfly carried the soul as a symbol of transformation, wisdom, and the parts of ourselves that only emerge once we've actually lived through something.

That's the idea underneath Syqei. Every butterfly you unlock isn't just a collectible. It's proof of a real moment lived, a small piece of your own becoming.

— Taleena
